1

To use this tool, first click on "Try It" and then you'll be redirected to the page tha does the calculations and generates the views.
In the "Prior Distribution" field, enter the prior values. Remember that the result of the sum must be 1.
Values can be fractions (such as 1/2, 1/3) or decimal numbers (such as 0.125, dot being the decimal separator). To separate the numbers from each other, use spaces or commas.
Click on "Set Matrix Values" to set the channel values.


‹ ›
2

Next, you can then enter the channel values. Each row represents an entry and the columns represent the outputs. The values must be formatted in the same way as the Prior Distribution.
Click "Visualize" for the system to provide the views and calculations, or click "Clear" to clear the screen.
If one of the entries is not in accordance with the format, a message will be alerted indicating the error and the values will be removed from the screen.


‹ ›
3

If the previous steps are correct, your visualization will be generated!



The image generated on the left can be rotated for better visualization of the distribution. The right image is the 2D mapping of the plane of the triangle that is shown in the previous figure.
Below them are the results of the intermediate steps used to generate the figures.
You can also choose to show or hide some points. Just click on the label indicated in the chart and the respective points will be hidden or shown.
In the figure on the right, you can also drag the green dot (representing the Prior) to anywhere within the triangle (such as the image below). The calculations will be redone, considering the new Prior values and the same channel values.
